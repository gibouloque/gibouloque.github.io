  • Short bio

    I am currently a post-doctoral researcher in the LinkMedia team at Renne's Inria Centre. My current research interests lie on emerging problems of AI security, notably problems regarding the identification of content produced by generative models, using both forensics and watermarking techniques. I am also interested in problems regarding the integrity of training and testing data (e.g. adversarial examples and backdoor attacks) and the way methods from the fields of information security and weak signal detections can applied to construct efficient defenses. I also worked as a post-doc at the the Czech Technical Univeristy within the Artificial Intelligence Center team. My research focused on information security, with particular attention to steganography and steganalysis and the ways to bridge the gap between academic research and real-world application. I used a variety of techniques within statistical hypothesis testing, game theory and signal processing for the modeling of real-world multimedia data and providing strong guarantees of performance.


    Before that, I was a PhD student at Troyes University of Technology under the supervision of Rémi Cogrannne and Patrick Bas. You can get my thesis manuscript on these.fr.


    I have had the pleasure to work with Pr. Jessica Fridrich's team at Binghamton University (NY) as a research scholar in 2018 and be part of their team during the ALASKA steganalysis challenge. Before this, I obtained a Master degree in Engineering Systems, Networks and Telecommunications with a specialization in Security for Communication Systems.
